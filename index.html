What is AI?

Neural Networks

AI is based on neural networks inspired by the human brain, with interconnected nodes that process information in layers.

Pattern Recognition

AI learns patterns from data and uses probability to predict the next word in a sequence, simulating human intelligence.

Large Language Models

Modern AI systems like ChatGPT are trained on vast amounts of text data from the internet, books, and articles.

Creative Generation

AI can generate human-like text, translate languages, write creative content, and answer questions informatively.

AI is not omniscient

Dare to try – Start small and experiment. You learn more by doing than waiting for perfect answers.

No magic – AI requires structure, data and people with direction. It's a tool, not a solution to everything.

Be curious – Ask questions, test ideas and use AI as a creative partner to explore new possibilities.

Change perspective – Let AI challenge your habit patterns and show new ways of looking at things.

Be patient – Good results require time, practice, and adjustments. It is a process, not a shortcut.

AI ignores truth – It finds patterns, not meaning. You must be responsible for interpretation and verification.

Key Concepts & Terminology

Understanding the fundamental building blocks of prompt engineering and AI language models.

Prompt

A specific instruction, question, or piece of text provided to an AI model to elicit a response or guide its output.

Prompts can range from simple questions to complex instructions with context, examples, and constraints. The quality of a prompt heavily influences the quality of the AI-generated output.

Large Language Model (LLM)

An advanced AI model trained on vast amounts of text data to understand, generate, and manipulate human language.

LLMs like GPT (Generative Pre-trained Transformer) are the engines behind many generative AI applications. They learn patterns and relationships in language to perform tasks like translation, summarization, and content creation.

Tokens

The basic units of text that LLMs process. A token can be a word, part of a word, or punctuation.

For example, the phrase "Prompt engineering is cool" might be tokenized into ["Prompt"], [" engineering"], [" is"], [" cool"]. LLMs have limits on the number of tokens they can process in a single prompt and response (context window).

Context Window

The maximum number of tokens an LLM can consider at one time, including both the input prompt and its generated output.

A larger context window allows the model to remember more information from earlier in a conversation or a longer piece of text, leading to more coherent and contextually relevant responses.

Temperature / Creativity

A parameter that controls the randomness of an LLM's output. Higher values lead to more creative and diverse responses, while lower values make the output more focused and deterministic.

For creative tasks like writing a story, a higher temperature might be preferred. For factual recall or summarization, a lower temperature is often better to reduce hallucinations or deviations.

How to Prompt Effectively

Who

Indicate your identity and desired AI persona for personalized answers

What

Describe the problem clearly to ensure relevant AI responses

How

Define expectations on AI responses and output format

Follow up

Ask questions and give feedback to refine AI responses

Advanced Prompting Techniques

  • Be specific: The more details you provide, the better the AI can tailor its response
  • Use examples: Show the AI what you want by including examples in your prompt
  • Break down complex tasks: Split complicated requests into smaller, manageable steps
  • Specify format: Tell the AI exactly how you want the information structured (bullet points, paragraphs, table, etc.)
  • Iterate and refine: Don't expect perfect results on the first try; use follow-up prompts to improve outputs

Common Prompting Mistakes

  • Being too vague: "Write something about business" vs. "Write a 500-word analysis of remote work trends in the tech industry since 2020"
  • Overloading with instructions: Trying to accomplish too many things in a single prompt
  • Not providing context: Failing to give the AI necessary background information
  • Ignoring iteration: Expecting perfect results without refining your prompts
  • Forgetting to specify audience: Not indicating who the content is for (technical experts, beginners, etc.)

Interactive Examples

Basic vs. Effective Prompt

Basic Prompt Not Recommended

Write about AI.

Too vague!

Artificial Intelligence (AI) refers to the simulation of human intelligence in machines that are programmed to think like humans and mimic their actions. The term may also be applied to any machine that exhibits traits associated with a human mind such as learning and problem-solving...

Generic, lacks focus and personalization

Effective Prompt Recommended

WHO: I am a team leader preparing a presentation for my colleagues.
WHAT: I need a concise explanation of AI that non-technical people can understand.
HOW: Please use simple analogies and limit to 3 key points. Include one example of AI in everyday business.
FOLLOW UP: I may need to adapt this for different departments.

Well structured!

Here's a simple explanation of AI for your presentation:

1. AI is like a student that learns from examples - Just as students learn from textbooks and practice, AI learns from data we provide it.

2. AI finds patterns humans might miss - Like how a financial analyst spots trends, but AI can process millions of transactions instantly.

3. AI automates repetitive tasks - Similar to how email filters sort your inbox, but for complex business processes.

Business Example: Customer service chatbots that handle common questions, freeing your team to focus on complex customer needs.

Targeted, concise, and practical
